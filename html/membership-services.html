keithsmith
2016-10-10 11:54
You are talking about a sybil attack by adding new peers when one membersrvc is compromised

keithsmith
2016-10-10 11:54
this will not be possible for the following reason

keithsmith
2016-10-10 11:57
The blockchain network is bootstrapped with multiple CA root certificates which everyone agrees upon and will be part of the genesis block. Also part of the genesis block is a policy for adding new CA root certificates. Let’s assume the policy is the unanimous policy.

keithsmith
2016-10-10 11:58
In order to add a new root CA certificate to the network, you must have the signature/approval of all existing CAs

keithsmith
2016-10-10 11:58
assuming “unanimous” policy to add new root CA certs

keithsmith
2016-10-10 12:03
Now, transactions are sent to peer’s yes, but the endorsement policy is based on a signature by a CA. So no matter how many peers there are associated with a CA / membership services / COP server, the endorsement policies count at the CA level, not at the peer level

keithsmith
2016-10-10 12:08
So in a nutshell, 2 things prevent a sybil attack:

keithsmith
2016-10-10 12:09
1) Endorsement policies count root CA signatures (aka “participants” in some of the doc), not peer signatures

keithsmith
2016-10-10 12:10
2) Adding new root CAs (aka “participants”) require approval by other CAs based upon some agreed upon policy

keithsmith
2016-10-10 12:10
I hope this makes sense
